üìå Notable Projects

PhD Thesis Exploring Knowledge Dynamics in Academia-Industry Collaborations within a Local Ecosystem

I examined knowledge production within academia-industry collaborations (AICs) in San Carlos de Bariloche, Argentina, using Boisot‚Äôs I-Space framework to characterize knowledge co-creation, and individual and organizational learning dynamics. Employing a mixed-methods approach, I integrated survey data with insights gathered from in-depth interviews to explore diverse modes of collaboration and their associated knowledge properties and learning processes. The findings provided strategic recommendations for stakeholders to enhance reciprocal learning and knowledge co-creation within AICs. Furthermore, by laying the groundwork for cross-institutional knowledge partnerships, this project contributes to shaping future strategies and policies in inter-institutional collaborations.

Network Analysis of Academic Collaborations

During my thesis project, I extensively explored social network analysis to measure information flows within academic collaborations. While initially intending to evaluate flows in academic-industry collaborations, I found that social network analysis was not the most suitable tool for this purpose. However, this experience allowed me to develop valuable skills in analyzing collaborations between academic organizations. Leveraging bibliometric databases, I conducted original analyses to visualize and analyze evolving networks of scholarly collaboration. This culminated in a presentation at the Networks 2021: Joint Sunbelt & NetSci Conference, where I shared insights about ‚ÄúScientific Collaboration Through Institutional Networks: The Value of National Partners.‚Äù Through this project, I gained a deeper understanding of academic collaboration dynamics and enhanced my proficiency in social network analysis techniques.

Development of Economic Indicators

My involvement in the development of economic indicators with CEPAL encompassed two primary projects. On the one hand, I contributed to the initial stages of designing and implementing a Consumer Price Index (CPI). This involved analyzing data to identify a representative consumption basket and calculating weights to ensure accurate inflation monitoring. On the other hand, I played a key role in estimating and updating coincident indicators to track the economic activity of Argentine sub-national states. This project entailed collecting new data on observable variables, deseasonalizing time series, running statistical models, and assessing data quality to inform strategic decision-making.
